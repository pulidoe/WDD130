History of the Potato in Peru

Family farming in Peru represents one of the most important and culturally relevant activities in our country, and the Potato is a fundamental axis of this family farming. The thousands of potato conservationist men and women have allowed us to maintain our agrobiodiversity. This Baseline must become an instrument that values the work they have developed over the years. Potatoes are the third most important food crop globally, after wheat and rice, due to their caloric intake, high production, and easy handling. Its long history of use by man begins more than 7000 years ago in the surroundings of Lake Titicaca, in southern Peru, where the ancient inhabitants started the domestication of wild potatoes, resulting in seven species and four subspecies of cultivated potatoes. , which to date include more than 4000 varieties. Additionally, there is a remarkable biodiversity of potatoes in our territory that reaches almost one hundred species, which are wild relatives of cultivated potatoes. Maintaining wild relatives and the habitat where they thrive is critical. Thus, the Peruvian Andes are the areas with the most significant genetic richness of potatoes globally, this crop being a strategic component of Andean agrobiodiversity.

Reaching Europe, Asia, and the World

After the arrival of the Spanish in America, the Potato was introduced into new territories (Europe and Asia) and is currently cultivated in more than one hundred countries. We must not forget that after it arrived in Europe and Asia, it saved millions of people from famine, and to date, these continents are the primary potato producers worldwide. Its value is so significant that countries on the Asian continent, such as China, currently the largest potato producer globally, where rice cultivation is so entrenched, are migrating areas from rice cultivation to potato cultivation, considered more nutritious and with greater efficiency. The Potato can get sick

The Irish were the first to recognize its high nutritional value. Potatoes became the staple food of the Irish in the early 1600s; most people depended on them for their existence. When late blight disease came to Ireland from the United States, it caused a national famine from 1845 to 1848, causing the death of almost a million people and the mass emigration abroad of a million more. The diversity of species and varieties of this crop provides us with more productive, pest-resistant, and resilient alternatives to face the effects of climate change that put food security at risk. Unfortunately, however, potato genetic resources, and agrobiodiversity in general, are being lost or degraded at an alarming rate due to the abandonment of the field and the lack of market opportunities and incentives for their cultivation.

The Potato in the United States

Potatoes were introduced to the United States in 1719 from supplies brought in from Ireland and were initially grown in New Hampshire. Today, the United States produces more than 420 million quintals (cwt) of potatoes annually on about 1.1 million acres, worth about $ 3.5 to 4.0. billion dollars. The Northeast states combined reach about 100,000 acres per year; Pennsylvania potato supplies are for processing (primarily for potato chips) and fresh produce (or eat-safe) markets. Eventually, perhaps in the early 1800s, the beloved Potato found its place in Thanksgiving traditions. So this holiday season, when you eat your mashed Potato, add Andean civilization to your thank you list.

The recent findings of Potato

In a study published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, archaeologists from the University of California report the discovery of direct evidence: microscopic remains of what appears to be Potato in ancient grinding tools from southern Peru. The remains date back to 3400 BC. C. The study authors looked for microscopic grains of starch in stone tools collected from an ancient highland site called Jiskairumoko in the Titicaca basin of southern Peru. They suspected that these tools were used to peel potatoes.

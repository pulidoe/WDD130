Reachinng Europe, Asia and the World

After the arrival of the Spanish in America, the Potato was introduced into new territories (Europe and Asia) and is currently cultivated in more than one hundred countries. We must not forget that after it arrived in Europe and Asia, it saved millions of people from famine, and to date, these continents are the primary potato producers worldwide. Its value is so significant that countries on the Asian continent, such as China, currently the largest potato producer globally, where rice cultivation is so entrenched, are migrating areas from rice cultivation to potato cultivation, considered more nutritious and with greater efficiency. Arriving to the US

Potatoes were introduced to the United States in 1719 from supplies brought in from Ireland and were initially grown in New Hampshire. Today, the United States produces more than 420 million quintals (cwt) of potatoes annually on about 1.1 million acres, worth about $ 3.5 to 4.0. billion dollars. The Northeast states combined reach about 100,000 acres per year; Pennsylvania potato supplies are for processing (primarily for potato chips) and fresh produce (or eat-safe) markets. Eventually, perhaps in the early 1800s, the beloved Potato found its place in Thanksgiving traditions. So this holiday season, when you eat your mashed Potato, add Andean civilization to your thank you list.

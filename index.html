Where the potato was borned

Potatoes are the third most important food crop globally, after wheat and rice, due to their caloric intake, high production, and easy handling. Its long history of use by man begins more than 7000 years ago in the surroundings of Lake Titicaca, in southern Peru, where the ancient inhabitants started the domestication of wild potatoes, resulting in seven species and four subspecies of cultivated potatoes. , which to date include more than 4000 varieties. Additionally, potatoes' remarkable biodiversity in our territory reaches almost one hundred species, which are wild relatives of cultivated potatoes. Maintaining wild relatives and the habitat where they thrive is critical. Thus, the Peruvian Andes are the areas with the most significant genetic richness of potatoes globally, this crop being a strategic component of Andean agrobiodiversity. food!

The Potato traveling around the world

After the arrival of the Spanish in America, the Potato was introduced into new territories (Europe and Asia) and is currently cultivated in more than one hundred countries. We must not forget that after it arrived in Europe and Asia, it saved millions of people from famine, and to date, these continents are the primary potato producers worldwide. Its value is so significant that countries on the Asian continent, such as China, currently the largest potato producer globally, where rice cultivation is so entrenched, are migrating areas from rice cultivation to potato cultivation, considered more nutritious and with greater efficiency.
